About Singapore

Singapore is ranked first in Lonely Planet's list of top 10 countries to visit in 2015. 
  Read More ->

Climate and Weather
It is generally warm and humid in Singapore through out the year. Its equatorial climate has no distinct seasons and temperature is usually around 30°C/86°F during the day and 23°C/74°F in the evening. Humidity is around 60% in the afternoon but higher in the morning. Weather reports and forecasts are readily available on the Internet or via mobile phones.
Lightweight cotton or quick-dry clothing is recommended. A coat or sweater can be useful as some air-conditioned venues can be a little cold.

Time
The official time in Singapore is UTC/GMT +8 and there is no daylight saving time. Sunrise and sunset in July are at around 0700hrs and 1915hrs, respectively.

Currency
The Singapore Dollar (SGD or S$) is the currency of Singapore. The current exchange rate is approximately
1 SGD = 0.7 USD = 0.65 EUR. 
Local Attractions
Singapore Zoological Gardens  (http://www.zoo.com.sg/)
With over 1.6 million worldwide visitors per year, the award-winning Singapore Zoological Gardens is a must see for tourists. Located in the northern part of Singapore, this 26-hectare wildlife park is the habitat to more than 2,800 animals representing over 300 species of mammals, birds, and reptiles. Visitors will be impressed by the immersive experience - many of the habitats in this zoo incorporates elevated platform, underwater galleries and glass observatories to gain fascinating perspective of the animals.

River Safari Singapore (http://www.riversafari.com.sg/)
Located next to the Singapore Zoological Gardens in the northern part of Singapore, River Safari is one of the newest wildlife attraction. This attraction is home to 6000 animals including 40 threatened species and features exploratory river adventure of the Mississippi, Congo, Nile, Ganges, Mekong and Yangtze rivers. The Giant Panda Forest exhibit is also the home of two pandas Kai Kai and Jia Jia. River Safari also boasts the world largest freshwater aquarium.

Jurong Bird Park Singapore (http://www.birdpark.com.sg/)
With a collection of more than 5000 birds across 400 species, the 20.2 hectares Jurong Bird Park, located in the western part of Singapore, is one of the world-renowned bird sanctuaries. The park houses the world largest walk-in aviary which houses 600 birds and a 30-meter high waterfall. The Breeding and Research Center allows visitors to understand how the park nurture the hatchlings into fine birds.

Sentosa Singapore (http://www.sentosa.com.sg/)
Located in the southern part of Singapore, Sentosa Island is connected to the mainland by a monorail and cable car. The island was used as a fort during 2nd World War and is now home to a few attractions including Universal Studios Singapore, Marine Life Park, Madame Tussauds Singapore and Underwater World Singapore. The Sentosa Fort Siloso offers visitors an historical insight into coastal defense in the Malayan region during World War II. The island also boasts three white sandy beaches for visitors to chill out.

Gardens by the Bay (http://www.gardensbythebay.com.sg/)
Gardens by the Bay captures the essence of Singapore as the premier Garden City where diverse living things co-exist in a first-world urban city. Located at the southern part of Singapore in the Marina Bay area, Gardens by the Bay offers close to 10 sub-attractions. The 1.2 hectares Flower Dome is an indoor conservatory that replicates the cool-dry climate of Mediterranean and semi-arid subtropical regions like South Africa and parts of Spain and Italy. Visitors will be able to discover plants and flowers from different corners of the globe. The Cloud Forest is a cool-moist conservatory which houses the world tallest indoor waterfall and is the home to plants from tropical highlands up to 2000 meters above the sea level.

Jurong Point Shopping Mall (http://www.jurongpoint.com.sg/)
Jurong Point is located beside the Boon Lay MRT station and is about 15 mins away from the Nanyang Executive Centre by bus or taxi. Open daily from 10 am to 10 pm, the 750,000 square feet shopping mall with 450 retailers is one of the largest in Singapore. Conference attendees may get souvenir from the many retailers in this shopping mall.

Food Culture (http://www.jurongpoint.com.sg/)
Singapore’s food is very much influenced by its diverse culture. With majority of the population being Chinese, Malays and Indians, eateries in Singapore offer wide variety of food to satisfy your palate. Typical local Chinese dish include the Hainanese Chicken Rice, Char Kway Teow, and Fish Ball Noodles. Malay dishes include Mee Rebus, Nasi Lemak, and Lontong while Indian dishes include Roti-prata and Mee Goreng. Immerse yourself into the local food culture by visiting food courts in any shopping malls in Singapore.
